# Mistral Slide Layouts

Use these extracted Mistral presentation patterns. The source reference deck is not bundled with this skill.

## Global Slide Frame

- Ratio: 16:9.
- Base canvas: beige light.
- Grid: subtle beige dark lines at low opacity.
- Margins: generous, usually 40-56 px in HTML output.
- Footer anchor: small M icon or section/page marker.
- Accent: rainbow bars, angular arrows, square blocks, or branded diagram parts.

## Core Deck Flow

1. Cover: black top band or full-bleed photo, rainbow bar stack, M icon/wordmark, title.
2. Agenda or section map: numbered modules 01-05 with strong grid alignment.
3. Section divider: large section number, short label, broad open space, branded accent.
4. Content slide: headline, subtitle, body grid, small M anchor.
5. Diagram/data slide: one central visual system plus concise annotation.
6. Photography slide: image-led with restrained text and logo treatment.
7. Closing slide: "Thank you" or direct call to action, logo, contact or next step.

## Content Patterns

### Headline With Body Grid

Use for explanation-heavy slides.

- Top: small subtitle label.
- Main: large headline on left or top.
- Body: 2, 3, or 4 columns of title plus paragraph.
- Accent: thin orange/red bar or small M icon.

### Branded Blocks

Use for concepts, capabilities, or product modules.

- Arrange square or rectangular blocks on the grid.
- Each block gets a short title and optional subtitle.
- Use black, beige medium, orange, and red sparingly.
- Keep corners square.

### Arrow Systems

Use for process, migration, or causal flow.

- Use thick angular arrows or stepped arrows.
- Prefer horizontal left-to-right flow.
- Use rainbow colors to signal sequence.
- Keep labels short and aligned.

### Diagram Slides

Use for systems, architecture, or frameworks.

- Use one central diagram, not many tiny diagrams.
- Combine black linework, beige fills, and 1-2 rainbow accent colors.
- Put explanatory notes around the diagram in small blocks.

### Illustration/Icon Slides

Use for feature sets or model/product families.

- Use monochrome icons for dense lists.
- Use color illustrations for hero concepts.
- Keep labels minimal.

### Pattern Backgrounds

Use sparingly for section breaks, cover variants, and high-level framing.

- Use grid nr 1 / grid nr 2 style: faint orthogonal lines over beige.
- Pair with strong black or rainbow accent.
- Do not place long paragraphs over busy patterns.

### Photography Covers

Use when the deck benefits from a real-world scene.

- Use actual relevant photography.
- Overlay black or beige text zones with high contrast.
- Add logo in a quiet, consistent location.

## Density Rules

- One dominant idea per slide.
- Max 4 columns for body content.
- Max 6 short bullets or 4 cards per normal content slide.
- If body copy exceeds roughly 90-120 words, split into another slide.
- If a diagram requires more than 5 labels, make a build sequence or multiple slides.
